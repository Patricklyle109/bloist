Solar is not just a feel-good purchase. It‚Äôs a power system upgrade: a hedge against rate increases, a resilience layer, and a practical way to reduce demand on power generators that drive pollution.

The technical argument (in plain language)

Electricity is produced on demand. When demand is high, grids often dispatch the most expensive and most polluting marginal generators. Distributed solar reduces that peak demand‚Äîespecially on sunny afternoons.

Combustion is where the pollution comes from. Fossil generation emits carbon dioxide and also local pollutants (NO‚Çì, SO‚ÇÇ, PM) that contribute to smog and health impacts.

Solar is a silent generator. No moving parts on the roof, no fuel deliveries, and predictable output curves you can model.

Key idea: marginal emissions

Even if a grid has clean baseline generation, the marginal plant serving extra load can be fossil. Cutting marginal load tends to cut marginal emissions.
